About Page

The formation of the group grew out of a meeting Gandalf had with Thorin in Bree which kindled Thorin's interest in recapturing his long lost family inheritance. (Thorin's grandfather had been the King under the Mountain when Smaug came and took it.) Remembering that he had once known an adventurous Hobbit on his travels in the Shire, Gandalf decided to add Bilbo to their company because he knew that stealth and cunning were preferable to force. Gandalf also believed that someone like Bilbo could keep the sometimes prideful and stubborn Dwarves from rash action. The superstitious Dwarves also considered thirteen to be an unlucky number, and as Gandalf had planned to leave on other business, welcomed a fourteenth to fill into their party.

Thorin Oakenshield

Thorin II was born in TA 2746 to the Dwarf prince Thráin II in the city of the Lonely Mountain. Early in his youth, Thorin and the other Dwarves of the Lonely Mountain were forced to flee by the dragon Smaug, in TA 2770. While in exile, he quickly grew into a capable warrior. This was demonstrated at the Battle of Azanulbizar, near Moria, in TA 2799. He fought with one of the Dwarven armies beneath Moria's East-gate, and at some point in the battle, his shield broke, and, using an oaken tree branch found on the ground as a shield, he gained the epithet "Oakenshield," which remained with him even in death.

Fili and Kili

Fíli and Kíli were brothers, the youngest of the thirteen dwarves who set out on Thorin Oakenshield's quest along with Bilbo Baggins to reclaim the Lonely Mountain from Smaug. They were also nephews of Thorin, sons of his sister Dís. They were close kin of Dain II Ironfoot, who would later become King under the Mountain after the death of Thorin in the Battle of Five Armies.

Fíli and Kíli were born in the Ered Luin in the year TA 2859. At the time of the quest, Fíli was 82, and Kíli was 77 years old. They were considered young for Dwarves, equivalent to being in their late twenties or early thirties in human terms.

Fíli and Kíli were the only Dwarves in the company to have lived their whole lives in the Ered Luin, and had never before travelled east of the Misty Mountains. They were also the only Dwarves in the company who had not fought in the Battle of Azanulbizar, as they were too young at the time.

Fíli and Kíli were described as being tall for Dwarves, with blue hoods and yellow beards. They were also said to be fair of face, as most of the members of Thorin's company were. They had the best eyesight and the keenest hearing of the Dwarves in the company, and were also the quickest to react. They were also the strongest of the Dwarves in the company, and were said to be as strong as any two other companions. They were also said to be as brave as any of the companions, and were the only ones to volunteer to enter the dragon's lair with Bilbo.
